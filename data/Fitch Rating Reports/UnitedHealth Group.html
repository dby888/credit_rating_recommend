Fitch Revises UnitedHealth's Outlook to Negative; Affirms IFS Ratings at 'AA-'

Fitch Ratings-Chicago-30 July 2025: Fitch Ratings has affirmed the 'AA-' (Very Strong) Insurer Financial Strength ratings of UnitedHealth Group Incorporated's (UnitedHealth) insurance company subsidiaries. In addition, Fitch has affirmed UnitedHealth's Long-Term Issuer Default Rating (IDR) and senior unsecured notes at 'A' and United HealthCare Services, Inc.'s (UHS) IDR at 'A'. The Rating Outlook has been revised to Negative from Stable.

The Outlook revision reflects new information disclosed by UnitedHealth in its second quarter earnings call on July 29. The company provided guidance on its significantly diminished operating performance for the remainder of 2025, which suggests that it will meet its financial leverage downgrade sensitivities for the year, with a partial recovery expected in 2026.

Key Rating Drivers

Weakened Operating Performance: Financial performance and earnings is a high influence key rating driver for UnitedHealth's ratings and was very strong until 2025. Operating margins and overall profitability are pressured this year due to continued unexpectedly high trends in healthcare costs, driven by both elevated utilization and unit costs. Based on management guidance, Fitch estimates that the operating EBITDA margin will be approximately 6.5% in 2025, down from 9.8% in 2024.

Pressure on Capitalization and Leverage: UnitedHealth's capitalization and leverage highly influence its ratings. Financial leverage metrics have consistently been in line with the rating sensitivities over the past decade but are likely to remain above guidelines for the current rating category in 2025 and possibly into 2026. Based on guidance provided by management in its July 29 earnings call, Fitch estimates that debt/EBITDA will be approximately 2.7x in 2025, up from approximately 2.0x in 2024, which represents the sensitivity for a potential downgrade, and the financial leverage ratio (FLR) will be approximately 43% at year-end.

Favorable Notching on Senior Notes: UnitedHealth's senior unsecured debt rating benefits from the magnitude of unregulated cash flows from the Optum operating segments and results in reduced notching from the IDR.

Risk-Based Capitalization: Fitch estimates the NAIC RBC ratio at YE 2024 for UnitedHealth's NAIC reporting operating subsidiaries at 230% of the company action level (CAL). This level of RBC remains below guidelines for the current rating category but is within management's stated target of 225%-250%. Capitalization remains supportive of the current ratings, and Fitch believes the organization has the willingness and the ability, if needed, to provide financial support to its various operating company subsidiaries.

Leading Position, Significant Scale: UnitedHealth is the largest U.S. health insurer and health services provider in terms of total medical membership. Fitch categorizes the company's business profile as "most favorable" under its rating criteria, and companies with this profile can reach a rating in the 'AA' category. The company's credit profile also benefits from significant cash flows derived from its Optum businesses that are less heavily regulated than health insurance.

Emerging Headwinds: Sector developments that may challenge UnitedHealth in its margin recovery efforts include ongoing elevated healthcare utilization trends, potentially higher acuity in the Medicaid risk pool driven by program changes contained in Trump's tax and spending bill signed earlier this month, and the likely expiration of enhanced Premium Tax Credits associated with the Affordable Care Act individual exchange business. The company has also recently acknowledged a Department of Justice investigation into aspects of the company's participation in the Medicare Advantage program. Fitch views the DOJ investigation as an event risk, the outcome of which is impossible to predict at this time.

RATING SENSITIVITIES

Factors that Could, Individually or Collectively, Lead to Negative Rating Action/Downgrade

--Average debt-to-EBITDA and financial leverage ratios exceeding 2.0x and 40%, respectively, on a six-quarter rolling basis;

--Sustained NAIC RBC ratio below 225% of the company action level;

--EBITDA to interest coverage below 10.0x;

--UnitedHealth's unsecured senior debt ratings could be downgraded if Optum's operating EBITDA relative to consolidated interest expense is less than approximately 5.0x or less than 30% of consolidated pretax operating income, or if it becomes encumbered by direct ownership of a regulated subsidiary.

Factors that Could, Individually or Collectively, Lead to Positive Rating Action/Upgrade

--Debt-to-EBITDA and FLRs below 1.4x and 30%, respectively, while maintaining a double-digit EBITDA-to-interest coverage ratio;

--NAIC risk-based capital ratio above 250% of the company action level.

Factors that Could, Individually or Collectively, Lead to a Stable Outlook

--Material improvement of FLR and debt/EBITDA ratio toward 40% and 2.0x, respectively.

Fitch Rates UnitedHealth's Senior Notes Issuance 'A'

Fitch Ratings-Chicago-17 June 2025: Fitch Ratings has assigned an 'A' rating to UnitedHealth Group Incorporated's (UNH) issuance of up to $3 billion of senior unsecured notes across four tranches (three-year, five-year, 10-year and 30-year). The existing ratings assigned to UNH and its subsidiaries are not affected by today's rating action.

Key Rating Drivers

Proceeds from the issuance are expected to be used primarily for repayment of near-term senior debt maturities. UNH has $2 billion of senior unsecured notes maturing on July 15, 2025, and $750 million of senior unsecured notes maturing on Oct. 15, 2025. The issuance is expected to be neutral to its financial leverage ratios, which were 2.0x for debt-to-EBITDA and 44% for the financial leverage ratio (FLR) at 1Q25.

The ratings assigned to the new senior notes are equivalent to Fitch's ratings on UNH's existing senior notes. The notes are rated at the same level as the company's Long-Term Issuer Default Rating (IDR) of 'A', which reflects the significant proportion of unregulated cash flow available to service debt.

UNH's Rating Outlook is Stable, reflecting its very strong company profile. UNH has a leading market position, considerable scale, strong diversification in terms of both earnings and geography, as well as a long history of very strong, consistent operating performance.

However, recent developments have created significant uncertainty about the company's near-term operating performance, potentially pressuring its ratings. UNH reduced and then suspended its 2025 earnings guidance due to unexpectedly high and worsening utilization trends. The resignation of the company's CEO, a string of unfavorable media reports, and shareholder litigation have further complicated matters.

Fitch will continue to monitor ongoing developments and compare UNH's developing profile with expectations for its ratings, principally as described in the rating sensitivities listed below. We will adjust the ratings or Outlooks as appropriate.

Factors that Could, Individually or Collectively, Lead to Negative Rating Action/Downgrade

--Sustained NAIC risk-based capital ratio below 225% of the company action level;

--EBITDA-to-interest coverage below 10x;

--UnitedHealth's unsecured senior debt ratings could be downgraded if Optum's operating EBITDA relative to consolidated interest expense is less than approximately 5x or less than 30% of consolidated pretax operating income, or if it becomes encumbered due to direct ownership by a regulated subsidiary.

Factors that Could, Individually or Collectively, Lead to Positive Rating Action/Upgrade

--Debt-to-EBITDA and FLRs below 1.4x and 30%, respectively, while maintaining a double-digit EBITDA-to-interest coverage ratio;

In addition, Fitch has affirmed UnitedHealth's Long-Term Issuer Default Rating (IDR) and senior unsecured notes at 'A', as well as the IDR of United HealthCare Services, Inc. The Rating Outlook is Stable.

The affirmation reflects UnitedHealth's current financial leverage metrics, which are slightly above rating expectations. Very strong operating performance and an industry-leading business profile continue to be supportive of the current ratings.

Key Rating Drivers

Strong Capitalization and Leverage: UnitedHealth's capitalization and leverage has a high influence on its ratings. Although temporarily elevated as a result of measures taken to deal with a cyberattack in early 2024, the company's financial leverage metrics have generally been consistent with rating sensitivities but remain above guidelines for the current rating category.

UnitedHealth's FLR and debt/EBITDA increased to approximately 43% and 2.1x, respectively, at YE24, up from 38% and 1.7x, respectively, at YE23. Outstanding debt increased in 2024 as the company borrowed to provide temporary funding to the provider community to offset slower claims payments resulting from the temporary shutdown of Change Healthcare following a cyberattack on its systems. Fitch expects the company to return to its run-rate leverage targets by YE25.

Favorable Notching on Senior Notes: UnitedHealth's senior unsecured debt rating benefits from the magnitude of unregulated cash flows generated by its Optum operating segments, resulting in reduced notching from the IDR.

Risk-Based Capitalization: Fitch estimates the combined NAIC RBC ratio at YE 2024 for UnitedHealth's NAIC reporting operating subsidiaries to be 240% of the company action level (CAL). This level of RBC remains below guidelines for the current rating category, but is in line with management's stated target of 225%-250%, and is supportive of the rating in conjunction with the company's other key rating drivers. Fitch believes the organization has the willingness and the ability, if needed, to provide financial support to its various operating company subsidiaries.

Very Strong Performance and Earnings: Financial performance and earnings highly influence UNH's ratings and remain supportive of the company's current rating category. Although non-recurring charges and expenses in 2024 drove a significant decline in net earnings, operating margin remains very strong and exhibits the inherent diversity and resiliency of the company's health benefits and health services operations. The company's operating EBITDA margin was approximately 9.0% in 2024, down from 9.8% in 2023.

In 1Q24, UnitedHealth completed the sale of its Brazil operations which include Amil Insurance, Amil Integrated Care, Amil Dental and Americas Hospitals. The sale resulted in a nonrecurring, pretax charge of approximately $8 billion. The majority of the charge was noncash and attributable to foreign currency translation losses.

Diversified Earnings: Operating earnings remain evenly split between the company's UnitedHealthcare and Optum segments in 2024, but revenue continued to grow at a faster rate in the less regulated Optum segment. Optum reported revenue growth of approximately 12% for 2024, compared with UnitedHealthcare's 6% increase. The Optum business platform contributed nearly $17 billion of pretax income in 2024, or roughly one-half of consolidated pretax income.

As a whole, the health insurance sector generated weaker operating performance in 2024, primarily reflecting continued increases in healthcare service utilization in the senior population and higher acuity in the remaining Medicaid population following completion of the redetermination process that began in April 2023. However, UnitedHealth's consolidated operating performance was less impacted by these pressures relative to many of its peers due primarily to the diversity of its products and services.

Leading Position, Significant Scale: UnitedHealth is the largest U.S. health insurer and health services provider in terms of total medical membership. Fitch categorizes the company's business profile as "most favorable" under its rating criteria, and companies with this profile can reach a rating in the 'AA' category. The company's credit profile also benefits from significant cash flows derived from its Optum businesses that are less heavily regulated than health insurance.

Key Rating Drivers

The rating actions reflect Fitch's assessment of the impact of the coronavirus pandemic, including its economic impact, under a set of rating assumptions described below. Fitch Ratings-Chicago-21 February 2020: Fitch Ratings has upgraded UnitedHealth Group Incorporated's (UNH) senior unsecured notes to 'A' from 'A-' and has affirmed UNH's Long-Term Issuer Default Rating (IDR) at 'A'. In addition, Fitch has affirmed the 'AA-' (Very Strong) Insurer Financial Strength ratings of UNH's insurance operating subsidiaries. The Rating Outlook is Stable. A complete list of rating actions follows at the end of this release.

Key Rating Drivers

The upgrade of UNH's senior unsecured debt recognizes the magnitude of unregulated cash flows from UNH's Optum operating segments and specifically reflects a change in recovery assumptions from those used by Fitch for insurance holding companies to those used for non-insurance corporate entities. Fitch expects that the majority of UNH's cash flows will be non-regulated going forward.

The new recovery assumption for UNH's unsecured senior debt is 'RR4' (average), up from 'RR5' (below average). Debt issue ratings with a 'RR4' recovery assumption are aligned with the IDR, whereas ratings with a 'RR5' are rated one notch below the IDR.

The affirmation of UNH's ratings reflects the company's strong capitalization and leverage profile, which remains consistent with rating sensitivities, but outside guidelines for the company's rating category. Very strong financial performance and an industry-leading business profile continue to be supportive of the current rating.

UNH's financial leverage as measured by debt/EBITDA and debt/capital remains higher than Fitch's guidelines for the current rating category, which range from 0.8x-1.7x and 10%-23%, respectively. Specifically, UNH finished 2019 with debt-to-EBITDA of 1.8x and a financial leverage ratio (FLR) of 40%. Fitch currently expects UNH to maintain average (on a six-quarter rolling basis) debt-to-EBITDA below 2.0x and financial leverage ratios of 40% or below.

The forecast for the combined NAIC RBC ratio at year-end 2019 for UNH's NAIC reporting operating subsidiaries is 243% of the company action level (CAL), which is in line with management's stated target of 225%-250%. Similar to financial leverage, capitalization is meeting rating sensitivities, but remains below guidelines for the current rating category, which are in excess of 275% of CAL. In terms of UNH's non-NAIC-reporting subsidiaries, Fitch believes the organization has the willingness and, if needed, the ability to provide financial support to its various operating company subsidiaries.

Very strong operating margins and overall stable profitability, as well as the inherent strength and diversity of the company's health benefits and health services operations, continue to support UNH's ratings. Profitability in 2019 remained consistent with both recent results and Fitch's guidelines for the current rating category, as seen in an EBITDA margin of 9.3% in 2019, besting in 8.9% three-year average.

Fitch ranks UNH's business profile as most favorable compared to that of all other U.S. health insurance companies. This ranking is based on its market-leading competitive position, very high diversification across business lines, geographies and distribution and significant cash flows derived from its Optum subsidiaries, which are generally less heavily regulated than those of health insurers. As a result, Fitch scores UNH's Business Profile at 'aa' under its credit factor scoring guidelines with a moderate influence on the IFS rating.

RATING SENSITIVITIES

Key rating sensitivities that could result in a downgrade include:

--Average debt-to-EBITDA and financial leverage ratios exceeding 2.0x and 40%, respectively, on a six quarter rolling basis;

--Sustained NAIC risk-based capital ratio below 225% of the company action level;

--EBITDA-to-interest coverage below 10x;

--Non-regulated cash flows to the parent company falling below half of total cash flows for an extended period or Fitch assessing that the quality of non-regulated cash flows has deteriorated significantly.

Key rating sensitivities that could result in an upgrade include:

--Debt-to-EBITDA and financial leverage ratios below 1.4x and 30%, respectively, while maintaining a double-digit EBITDA-to-interest coverage ratio;

--NAIC risk-based capital ratio above 250% of the company action level.
