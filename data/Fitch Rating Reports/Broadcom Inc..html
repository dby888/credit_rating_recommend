Fitch Rates Broadcom's Senior Notes Issue 'BBB'

Fitch Ratings-Chicago-07 July 2025: Fitch Ratings has assigned to Broadcom Inc.'s proposed senior notes issue a 'BBB' rating. The notes will rank pari passu with Broadcom's existing and future senior unsecured obligations. Broadcom will use net proceeds for general corporate purposes, which may include organic investments, debt repayment and acquisitions.

Key Rating Drivers

Strengthened Financial Flexibility: Positive operating momentum from strong demand for artificial intelligence (AI) and VMware's virtualization products enhances financial flexibility. Free cash flow (FCF) will be higher and more stable due to the increasing mix of recurring maintenance and support net revenue associated with the software portfolio, which doubled to 42% in FY24 (year end Nov. 3, 2024) from FY23 with the VMware Inc. acquisition. Fitch forecasts $15 billion-20 billion of annual FCF and expanded capacity for capital returns and acquisitions without weakening the balance sheet.

More Conservative Leverage Profile: EBITDA leverage should remain below Fitch's 2.0x positive leverage sensitivity over the longer term with only temporary increases to accommodate partly debt-funded acquisitions. Fitch expects Broadcom will structure acquisition funding to return EBITDA leverage to within the agency's rating sensitivities in the 12-24 months after deal close, consistent with the company's historical approach, including its most recent purchase of VMware. Meanwhile, cash flow from operations (CFO) less capex/debt should average more than 40%.

Improved Revenue Growth Profile: Broadcom's revenue profile has strengthened from prior years. Broadcom's expansion of its hyperscaler customer set due to robust demand for AI infrastructure build-outs will drive long-term net revenue growth, although potential excess capacity at customers in the medium run may present a risk. Broadcom's more strategic engagement with Apple Inc. will benefit semiconductor net revenue while reducing product volatility. Meanwhile, maintenance and support revenue in the infrastructure software segment supports more stable net revenue growth.

Industry-Leading Profit Margins: Broadcom's strong positions and high share of net revenue from its software portfolio result in the highest profit margins among similarly rated semiconductor peers. Fitch expects EBITDA margins of roughly 60% through the forecast horizon and more stable EBITDA from higher recurring maintenance and support revenue associated with software. This net revenue mix also reduces capital intensity further, given Broadcom's largely outsourced manufacturing model, and strengthens FCF.

Short-Term Ratings: The 'F2' short-term ratings reflect Broadcom's strengthened FCF profile in conjunction with recent refinancing to improve liquidity through the rating horizon and Fitch's increased confidence in the company's commitment to higher long-term ratings. As a result, Fitch's financial flexibility rating factor for Broadcom is 'bbb+', supporting the 'F2' short-term ratings.

Peer Analysis

Broadcom's operating profile is stronger than those of its peers, including Micron Technology Inc. (BBB/Stable), which is more cyclical and capital-intensive but also more conservatively capitalized. Broadcom's greater financial flexibility and stronger profitability than NXP Semiconductors N.V. (BBB+/Stable) offset NXP's comparatively conservative financial policies.

Broadcom's less conservative financial structure than Marvell Technology Inc. (BBB/Stable) is offset by its leading market position and greater diversification. Broadcom's financial policies are more opportunistic than Microchip Technology, Inc. (BBB/Stable), but are offset by higher market share and diversification and a stronger FCF profile.

Key Assumptions

--High-teens organic revenue growth in FY25 before moderating to mid-single-digit organic revenue growth in FY26-FY27

--Stable profit margins with cost savings from VMware integration offsetting slightly lower profit margins associated with sales to hyperscalers

--Excess cash used for share repurchases

--Upcoming debt maturities refinanced and future acquisitions structured to sustain EBITDA leverage below 2.0x over the long term

RATING SENSITIVITIES

Factors that Could, Individually or Collectively, Lead to Negative Rating Action/Downgrade

--Expectations of EBITDA leverage above 2.5x beyond 12-24 months following a leveraging acquisition or CFO less capex approaching 20% of debt

--Expectations of organic revenue declines or market share losses from aggressive cost-cutting

Factors that Could, Individually or Collectively, Lead to Positive Rating Action/Upgrade

--EBITDA leverage sustained below 2.0x and CFO less capex/debt sustained above 30%

--Continued end-market diversification that offsets risks associated with exposure to Apple Inc

Liquidity and Debt Structure

Broadcom's liquidity is adequate and, as of May 4, 2025, was supported by $9.5 billion of cash and cash equivalents and a $4 billion commercial paper program, which was nearly fully drawn at quarter-end. The CP program is more than fully backstopped by an undrawn $7.5 billion revolving credit facility expiring January 2030. Fitch's forecast for $15 billion-20 billion of annual FCF also supports liquidity.

Issuer Profile

Broadcom is a leading provider of wired and wireless semiconductor solutions and infrastructure software, including virtualization and cloud computing software.

The senior notes will be pari passu with Broadcom's existing and future senior unsecured indebtedness. The company will use net proceeds to repay a portion of outstanding term loans related to the VMware, Inc. acquisition and for general corporate purposes.

Fitch estimates VMware's FY24 net revenue was $12.5 billion, exceeding expectations, while positive bookings momentum points to solid FY25 net revenue growth. Meanwhile, Broadcom achieved its target reduction of quarterly VMware operating expenses to $1.2 billion in FY24Q4 from more than $2.4 billion pre-acquisition; however, this includes cuts to product development.

Improved Revenue Growth Profile: Broadcom's revenue profile has strengthened from prior years. Higher ST Ratings: The upgrade of the ST ratings to 'F2' from 'F3' reflects Broadcom's strengthened FCF profile in conjunction with recent refinancing activities to improve liquidity through the rating horizon and Fitch increased confidence in the company's commitment to higher LT ratings. As a result, Fitch upgraded Fitch's Financial Flexibility rating factor for Broadcom to 'bbb+' from 'bbb', supporting the higher of the two ST ratings.

Broadcom's operating profile is stronger than those of its peers, including Micron Technology Inc. (BBB/Stable), which is more cyclical and capital-intensive but also more conservatively capitalized. Compared with Microchip Technology, Inc. (BBB/Stable), Broadcom's financial policies are more opportunistic, but are offset by higher market share and diversification and a stronger FCF profile.

Fitch Ratings-Chicago-20 December 2024: Fitch Ratings has affirmed the Long-Term Issuer Default Rating (IDR) and senior unsecured ratings for Broadcom Inc. and its wholly-owned subsidiaries Broadcom Technology Inc., CA Inc. and VMware LLC at 'BBB'. Fitch has also upgraded the short-term (ST) IDR and commercial paper (CP) ratings for Broadcom Inc. to 'F2' from 'F3'. Fitch has revised the Rating Outlook for the affirmed ratings to Positive from Stable.

Broadcom Inc.'s ratings and Outlook reflect the company's strengthening financial profile underpinned by higher and more stable profitability growth that increases Broadcom's flexibility for acquisitions without stressing the balance sheet and will drive EBITDA leverage close to Fitch's 2.0x positive leverage sensitivity in the near-term.

Key Rating Drivers

Strengthened Financial Flexibility: Positive operating momentum from strong demand for artificial intelligence (AI) and VMware's virtualization products is strengthening financial flexibility. FCF will be higher and more stable due to the increasing mix of recurring maintenance and support net revenue associated with the software portfolio, which doubled to 42% in FY24 from FY23 with the VMware Inc. acquisition. Fitch forecasts $15 billion-$20 billion of annual FCF, up from more than $10 billion previously and expanded capacity for capital returns and acquisitions without weakening the balance sheet.

More Conservative Leverage Profile: Broadcom should approach Fitch's 2.0x positive leverage sensitivity in the near-term and maintain EBITDA leverage below 2.0x over the longer-term with only temporary increases to accommodate partly debt-funded acquisitions. As the company has historically and most recently with VMware, Fitch expects Broadcom will structure acquisition-funding to return EBITDA leverage to within Fitch's rating sensitivities in the 12 months-24 months post-deal close. Meanwhile, CFO-capex/debt should average more than 40%, up from the mid- to high-30% more recently.

Reduced VMware Integration Risk: Broadcom has largely integrated the VMware acquisition, Broadcom's largest ever, but concerns over the impact of spending cuts on long-term innovation remain. Fitch estimates VMware's FY24 net revenue was $12.5 billion, exceeding expectations while positive bookings momentum point to solid FY25 net revenue growth. Meanwhile, Broadcom achieved its target reduction of quarterly VMware operating expenses to $1.2 billion in FY24Q4 from more than $2.4 billion pre-acquisition but this includes cuts to product development.

Improved Revenue Growth Profile: Broadcom's revenue profile has strengthened from prior years. Broadcom's expansion of its hyperscaler customer set amidst robust demand for AI infrastructure build-outs will drive long-term positive net revenue growth, although Fitch remains concerned over potential excess capacity at customers in the medium-run. Broadcom's more strategic engagement with Apple Inc. will benefit semiconductor net revenue while reducing product volatility. Fitch Ratings-Chicago-05 August 2024: Fitch Ratings has upgraded the Long-term Issuer Default Rating (IDR) and senior unsecured ratings for Broadcom Inc. and its wholly-owned subsidiaries Broadcom Technology Inc., CA Inc. and VMware LLC to 'BBB' from 'BBB-'. Fitch has also affirmed the Short-term IDR and commercial paper ratings for Broadcom Inc. at 'F3'. The Rating Outlook is Stable.

The ratings and Outlook reflect Broadcom's increasing financial flexibility. Fitch expects Broadcom to structure ongoing acquisitions to return leverage metrics to levels in-line with the upgraded rating. Increased diversification and ongoing execution against VMware-related target synergies will strengthen financial performance through the rating horizon.

Key Rating Drivers

Strengthened Financial Flexibility: Fitch expects Broadcom's increased mix of software infrastructure net revenue and execution on VMware acquisition-related targets to strengthen its financial flexibility. With software net revenue, pro forma for VMware, accounting for more than 40% of total, Fitch forecasts $10 billion-$15 billion of less cyclical annual FCF through the forecast, supporting sizeable incremental debt-funded acquisitions and opportunistic share repurchases at the current rating.

Improved Financial Discipline: Fitch believes Broadcom will sustain leverage metrics in-line with the rating over the long-term despite temporary weakening of leverage metrics to support its acquisition strategy. We expect Broadcom will structure incremental deal funding such that the company can continue using FCF for sufficient debt reduction to return EBITDA leverage back below 2.5x within 12-24 months post-acquisition close, as it did with the VMware acquisition.

Fitch's negative EBITDA leverage rating sensitivity is 2.5x, but Broadcom's EBITDA leverage has been a Fitch-estimated 1.8x and 2.0x for fiscal years 2023 and 2022, respectively. We believe this is largely due to the impact of a higher interest environment on Broadcom's deal pipeline. Fitch assesses Broadcom's leverage tolerance based on the company's financial policies framework. However, an incremental positive rating action would most likely require management's public articulation of a long-term leverage target.

Acquisitive Nature: Fitch expects Broadcom will remain acquisitive in the infrastructure software space due to industry fragmentation and lower political and regulatory risk compared with semiconductors. The company's track record suggests that debt-financed mid- to large-scale acquisitions are more likely than organically funded tuck-in deals. Fitch still expects Broadcom to use post-deal FCF to return credit metrics to within long-term ranges consistent with the current rating over the subsequent 12 months-24 months.

VMware Acquisition: The acquisition of VMware was Broadcom's largest deal to date and meaningfully diversified its infrastructure software segment with the addition of faster-growing cloud-focused revenue. The combination increased the mix of software revenue to more than 40% of company-wide net revenue, versus just over 20% pre-deal, improving visibility with greater recurring maintenance and support sales. Integration is on track but still at risk from aggressive cuts to product development spending required to achieve Broadcom's synergy targets.

Stable Growth Profile: Fitch expects secular growth in enterprise, hyperscale and service providers, as well as infrastructure software maintenance to drive solid average revenue growth over the long term. VMware increased recurring revenue and enabled cross-selling. Broadcom's semiconductor solutions segment will benefit over the near term from hyperscale spending on artificial intelligence infrastructure and the company's strengthened relationships with leading smartphone makers.

High Revenue Diversification: Fitch believes Broadcom's revenue diversification is high for the semiconductor sector, resulting in lower operating cyclicality than its semiconductor peers. The addition of VMware will roughly double the infrastructure software net revenue mix to more than 40% of total, even after accounting for strong semiconductor solutions revenue growth from robust AI spending. The subscription revenue associated with software is more stable than that of the semiconductor segment, particularly given Broadcom's significant merchant components businesses.

These factors offset Broadcom's increased concentration to Apple Inc., which constituted 20% of net revenue in fiscal 2023 and 2022. Fitch also expects robust demand for AI infrastructure will increase concentration to hyperscaler customers who account for the vast majority of spending on AI, although Broadcom is engaged with these customers on a multi-generational strategic basis.

Strong Profit Margins: Broadcom's profit margins should remain among the industry's highest with EBITDA margins in the low-60%, due to a high mix of software assets and leading positions across a broad set of higher-value semiconductor solutions offerings. The company's software portfolio has also reduced profitability cyclicality due to considerable recurring maintenance and support revenue. Broadcom's integration of VMware is on track to achieve $4.4 billion of run rate operating expense savings in fiscal 2025, converging VMware's operating profit margins with that of classic Broadcom.

Derivation Summary

Broadcom's operating profile is stronger than those of its peers, including Micron Technology Inc. (BBB/Stable), which is more cyclical and capital-intensive but also more conservatively capitalized. Compared with NXP Semiconductors N.V. (BBB+/Stable), Broadcom's greater financial flexibility and stronger profitability offset NXP's comparatively conservative financial policies. Compared with Marvell Technology Group Ltd. (BBB-/Positive), Broadcom's less conservative financial structure is offset by its leading market position and greater diversification relative to Marvell. Compared with Microchip Technology, Inc. (BBB/Stable), Broadcom's financial policies are more opportunistic, but are offset by its greater diversification and free cash flow profile.

Parent-Subsidiary Linkage Assessment: Under Fitch's Parent-Subsidiary Linkage criteria, we equalize the ratings for Broadcom and its subsidiaries, including VMware. After determining that a parent-subsidiary linkage between Broadcom and VMware exists, Fitch follows the stronger subsidiary/weaker parent path. The absence of legal ring fencing in VMware's bond indentures and Broadcom's access and control, despite the company's intention to let VMware operate on a standalone basis, support the case that Broadcom has access to VMware's cash flow.

Key Assumptions

--Low double-digit organic revenue growth in fiscal 2024 and total revenue growth of roughly 40% with the acquisition of VMware;

--Low double-digit total revenue growth in fiscal 2025 from strong AI demand and semiconductor end market recovery;

--Mid-single-digit revenue growth in the out years;

--Realization of VMware cost synergies and a richer sales mix driving modest profit margin expansion through the forecast period;

--Broadcom uses excess cash for share repurchases;

--Broadcom refinances upcoming debt maturities and structures future acquisitions with an aim to sustain EBITDA leverage in the 2.0x-2.5x range over the long-term.

RATING SENSITIVITIES

Factors That Could, Individually or Collectively, Lead to Positive Rating Action/Upgrade

--Broadcom's public commitment to maintaining EBITDA leverage below 2.0x;

--Continued end market diversification that offsets risks associated with exposure to Apple.

Factors That Could, Individually or Collectively, Lead to Negative Rating Action/Downgrade

--Expectations for EBITDA leverage to be sustained above 2.5x beyond 12-24 months following a leveraging acquisition;

--Expectations for negative organic revenue growth or market share losses from aggressive cost cutting.

Liquidity and Debt Structure

Solid Liquidity: Fitch expects liquidity will remain solid for the rating and, as of May 5, 2024, was supported by $9.8 billion of cash and cash equivalents and an undrawn $7.5 billion revolving credit facility expiring Jan. 19, 2026, which backstops the company's $2.0 billion CP program. Fitch's forecast for $10 billion of annual FCF also supports liquidity.

Broadcom is a leading provider of wired and wireless semiconductor solutions and infrastructure software, including virtualization and cloud computing software with the acquisition of VMware, Inc.

Fitch Ratings-Chicago-29 November 2023: Fitch Ratings has affirmed at 'BBB-' the Long-Term (LT) Issuer Default Rating (IDR) and senior unsecured ratings for Broadcom, Inc. and its wholly-owned subsidiaries, Broadcom Technology, Inc. and CA, Inc. Fitch has also affirmed the Short-Term IDR and commercial paper (CP) ratings for Broadcom, Inc. at 'F3'. Fitch has resolved the Rating Watch Negative on VMware, Inc. following the Nov. 22, 2023 close of Broadcom's acquisition of VMware, and downgraded the LT IDR and senior unsecured ratings to 'BBB-' from 'BBB'. The Rating Outlook for Broadcom and its wholly-owned subsidiaries, which now includes VMware, is Stable.

The ratings and Outlook reflect Broadcom's strong financial flexibility and leading positions across a diversified set of end markets, including recurring software revenue accounting for more than 20% of consolidated revenue. Its opportunistic financial policies will support debt-funded acquisitions and capital returns with FCF, constraining positive rating actions absent a public commitment to tighter long-term leverage. Pro forma for the VMware acquisition close, Fitch estimates EBITDA leverage is approximately 3.0x, excluding projected cost synergies.

The downgrade of VMware's IDR reflects Fitch's assessment that the parent-subsidiary linkage between the stronger subsidiary, VMware, and weaker parent, Broadcom, is open due to a lack of legal ring fencing and Broadcom's access and control. As a result, Fitch believes Broadcom has full access to VMware's cash flows, which supports the equalization of the ratings at 'BBB-'.

Key Rating Drivers

VMware Acquisition: The acquisition of VMware strengthens Broadcom's operating profile by diversifying its infrastructure software segment with the addition of $13 billion of faster-growing cloud-focused revenue. The combination also increases the mix of software revenue to more than 40% of company-wide revenue, versus just over 20% pre-deal, improving visibility with greater recurring maintenance and support sales.

Broadcom funded the $69 billion acquisition, which includes $8 billion net debt assumed from VMware, in part with $30 billion of term loans. Fitch estimates pro forma EBITDA leverage at approximately 3x but approaching the company's 2.5x longer-term gross leverage target in fiscal 2025.

Risks include the potential for aggressive cost cuts, given VMware's lower than industry average profit margins from significant product development spending. Broadcom is targeting growing operating EBITDA to $8.0 billion from roughly $4.5 billion over the 12 months-24 months post-close, much of it from cost cuts rather than accelerating revenue growth. VMware's spending is higher than that of its peers, but Fitch believes spending has been instrumental to VMware's rapid growth and reinforcing the company's market leadership positions.

Parent-Subsidiary Linkage Assessment: Under Fitch's Parent-Subsidiary Linkage criteria, we equalize the ratings for Broadcom and its subsidiaries, including VMware. After determining that a parent-subsidiary linkage between Broadcom and VMware exists, Fitch follows the stronger subsidiary/weaker parent path. The absence of legal ring fencing in VMware's bond indentures and Broadcom's access and control, despite the company's intention to let VMware operate on a standalone basis, support the case that Broadcom has access to VMware's cash flow.

Acquisitive Nature: Fitch expects Broadcom will remain acquisitive in the infrastructure software space, given still considerable industry fragmentation and lower political and regulatory risk compared with that of semiconductors. The company's track record suggests that mainly debt-financed mid- to large-scale acquisitions are more likely than organically funded tuck-in deals. Nonetheless, Fitch expects Broadcom to use post-deal FCF to return credit metrics to within long-term ranges consistent with investment-grade ratings over the subsequent 12 months-24 months.

Public Financial Policies: Fitch has forecast $10 billion of annual FCF for the company, which should support post-deal debt reduction even as the company focuses on growing its dividend and repurchasing common shares. Nonetheless, Fitch expects the company will defend its investment-grade ratings, as it has done in the past and this transaction by issuing equity.

Fitch estimates EBITDA leverage closer to 2.0x for the latest 12 months ended July 30, 2023 and expects the company has the capacity to take gross leverage back to its long-term target of 2.5x from a Fitch-estimated 3.0x, pro forma for the deal. Over the long term, Fitch expects the company will have headroom to support incremental acquisitions under Fitch's 3.0x negative sustained gross leverage sensitivity for tuck-in acquisitions or capital returns.

Stable Growth Profile: Fitch expects solid organic revenue growth for Broadcom driven by secular growth in enterprise, hyperscale and service provider, as well as infrastructure software maintenance. The VMware transaction provides upside for the company's software business by increasing software sales mix to more than 40% of total revenue, increasing recurring revenue and enabling cross-selling opportunities.

Software Revenue Diversification: Broadcom's increasingly diversified revenue portfolio should reduce longer-term operating volatility. Its increasing revenue exposure in subscriptions and services, which Fitch estimates is roughly a third of total, is highly recurring and meaningfully offsets product-driven volatility associated with Broadcom's consumer business. Broadcom's software acquisitions have also increased predictability of profiles, given considerable recurring maintenance and support revenue.

Fitch believes Broadcom's credit profile is solid for the 'BBB-' rating with its strong operating profile offset by a track record for debt-funded acquisitions weighing on credit metrics. Fitch has also affirmed the 'F3' Short-Term IDR and commercial paper (CP) ratings for Broadcom, Inc. The Rating Outlook is Stable.

The ratings and Outlook reflect Broadcom's strong financial flexibility and leading positions across a diversified set of end markets, including recurring software revenue accounting for more than 20% of consolidated revenue. Its opportunistic financial policies will support largely debt-funded acquisitions, including the potential $69 billion VMware, Inc. deal and capital returns with FCF and constrain positive rating actions absent a public commitment to tighter long-term leverage.

Key Rating Drivers

VMware Acquisition: Broadcom's acquisition of VMware, if approved by regulators, will strengthen the acquirer's operating profile by diversifying its infrastructure software segment with the addition of $13 billion of faster-growing cloud-focused revenue. The combination will also result in software revenue representing more than 40% of company-wide revenue, versus just over 20% pre-deal, improving visibility with greater recurring maintenance and support sales.

Broadcom will partially fund the $69 billion acquisition, which includes $8 billion net debt assumed from VMware, with $30 of term loans. The closing is still subject to customary regulatory approvals. Fitch forecasts pro forma EBITDA leverage at about mid-3x but approaching the company's 2.5x longer-term gross leverage target in fiscal 2025.

Risks include the potential for aggressive cost cuts, given VMware's lower than industry average profit margins from significant product development spending. Broadcom is targeting growing operating EBITDA to $8.5 billion from roughly $5.0 billion over the 12-24 months post-close, much of it from cost cuts rather than accelerating revenue growth. VMware's spending is higher than that of its peers, but Fitch believes spending has been instrumental to VMware's rapid growth and reinforcing the company's market leadership positions.

Acquisitive Nature: Fitch expects Broadcom will remain acquisitive in the infrastructure software space, given still considerable industry fragmentation and lower political and regulatory risk compared with that of semiconductors. The company's track record suggests that mainly debt-financed mid- to large-scale acquisitions are more likely than organically funded tuck-in deals. Nonetheless, Fitch expects Broadcom to use post-deal FCF to return credit metrics to within long-term ranges consistent with investment-grade ratings over the subsequent 12-24 months.

Public Financial Policies: Fitch has forecast $10 billion of annual FCF for the company, which should support post-deal debt reduction even as the company focuses on growing its dividend and repurchasing common shares. Nonetheless, Fitch expects the company will defend its investment-grade ratings, as it has done in the past by issuing preferred equity.

Fitch estimates EBITDA leverage closer to 2.0x for the latest 12 months ended July 30, 2023 and expects the company has the capacity to take gross leverage back to its long-term target of 2.5x from the estimated mid-3.0x, pro forma for the deal. Over the long term, Fitch expects the company will have headroom to support incremental acquisitions under Fitch's 3.0x negative sustained gross leverage sensitivity for tuck-in acquisitions or capital returns.

Stable Growth Profile: Fitch expects solid organic revenue growth for Broadcom driven by secular growth in enterprise, hyperscale and service provider, as well as infrastructure software maintenance. Fitch Ratings-Chicago-01 June 2022: Fitch Ratings has affirmed the long-term 'BBB-' and short-term 'F3' ratings for Broadcom, Inc. and the 'BBB-' long-term ratings for its wholly-owned subsidiaries, Broadcom Technology, Inc. and CA, Inc. on the company's announcement that it reached a definitive agreement to acquire VMware, Inc. The Rating Outlook is Stable.

The ratings and Outlook reflect Broadcom's solid financial flexibility and Fitch's expectation that the company will bring credit metrics back in-line with the 'BBB-' rating in the 12-24 months following the deal's close.

Key Rating Drivers

VMware Acquisition: Broadcom will partially fund the $69 billion acquisition, which includes $8 billion net debt assumed from VMware, with $32 billion of new fully-committed debt financing from a consortium of banks. The closing (currently anticipated for mid-2023) is subject to customary closing conditions and regulatory approvals. Fitch forecasts pro forma total debt to operating EBITDA (gross leverage) at about mid-3x but approaching the company's 2.5x longer-term gross leverage target in fiscal 2025.

From a strategic perspective, VMware will strengthen Broadcom's operating profile by adding nearly $13 billion of faster growing cloud annual revenue to Broadcom's largely on-premise slower growing software franchises. The combination will also result in software revenue representing nearly half of company-wide revenue, versus just over a third pre-deal, improving visibility with greater recurring maintenance and support sales.

At the same time, risks include the potential for aggressive cost cuts, given VMware's lower than industry average profit margins from significant product development spending. Broadcom is targeting growing operating EBITDA to $8.5 billion from $4.7 billion for the fiscal year ended Jan. 31, 2022, much of it from cost cuts rather than just accelerating revenue growth. VMware's spending is higher than that of its peers, but Fitch believes spending has been instrumental to VMware's rapid growth and reinforcing the company's market leadership positions, which aggressive rationalization could weaken.

The pace of cost reductions will be instrumental to the combined company's deleveraging story, given that Broadcom simultaneously announced that its board of directors authorized another share repurchases program of up to $10 billion through fiscal 2023. However, Fitch expects the company would ultimately curtail stock buybacks to defend investment-grade ratings should demand conditions worsen meaningfully. Broadcom's earnings release pointed to a strong back half of fiscal 2022 and visibility into revenue growth in fiscal 2023, positioning the company for supporting both debt reduction and share repurchases.

Acquisitive Nature: Fitch expects Broadcom will remain acquisitive in the infrastructure software space, given still considerable industry fragmentation and lower political and regulatory risk compared with that of semiconductors. The company's track record suggests that mainly debt-financed mid- to large-scale acquisitions are more likely than organically funded tuck-in deals. Nonetheless, Fitch expects Broadcom to use post-deal FCF to return credit metrics to within long-term ranges consistent with investment grade ratings over the subsequent 12-24 months.

Public Financial Policy: Fitch's forecast $7 billion to $10 billion of annual FCF, which should support post-deal debt reduction even as the company focuses on growing its dividend and repurchasing common shares. Nonetheless, Fitch expects the company will defend its investment-grade ratings, as it has done in the past by issuing preferred equity.

Meanwhile, Fitch estimates total leverage (total debt to operating EBITDA) was 2.3x for the latest 12 months ended May 1, 2022, and expects the company has capacity to take gross leverage back to its long-term target of 2.5x from the estimated mid-3.0x, pro forma for the deal. Over the long term, Fitch expects the company will have headroom to support incremental acquisitions under Fitch's 3.0x negative sustained gross leverage sensitivity for tuck-in acquisitions or capital returns.

Stable Growth Profile: Fitch expects solid organic revenue growth for Broadcom in fiscal 2022 driven by secular growth in enterprise, hyperscale and service provider, as well as infrastructure software maintenance. Supply constraints remain a concern more broadly, particularly within the context of ongoing supply chain constraints, but Fitch believes secular growth drivers bode well for demand over the longer term. The VMware transaction provides upside for the company's software business by increasing software sales mix to nearly 50% of total revenue, increasing recurring revenue and enabling cross-selling opportunities.

Software Revenue Diversification: Broadcom's increasingly diversified revenue portfolio should reduce longer-term operating volatility. Its increasing revenue exposure in subscriptions and services, which Fitch estimates is roughly a third of total, is highly recurring and meaningfully offsets product-driven volatility associated with Broadcom's consumer business. Meanwhile, its semiconductor product exposure is largely to data center (DC) and service providers, providing meaningful customer and end-market diversification, as well as longer lifecycle products such as broadband and set-top boxes.

Strong Profit Margins: Broadcom's profit margins are among the industry's highest, due to software assets and leading positions across a broad set of infrastructure markets and wired infrastructure markets, such as ethernet switching, fiber-optic components and set-top boxes. Broadcom's software acquisitions have also increased predictability of profiles, given considerable recurring maintenance and support revenue.

Derivation Summary

Fitch believes Broadcom is solid for the 'BBB-' rating with its strong operating profile offset by a track record for debt-funded acquisitions weighing on credit metrics. The company's robust profit margins, end-market and customer diversification and leading market share positions support its operating profile strength. Meanwhile, higher profitability and low capital intensity are driving FCF growth and strengthening the financial profile, even as incremental deals are likely to be mainly debt-funded.

Broadcom's operating profile is stronger than those of its peers, including Micron Technology Inc. (BBB/Stable), which is more cyclical and capital-intensive but also more conservatively capitalized. Compared with NXP Semiconductors N.V. (BBB/Stable), Broadcom's greater financial flexibility and stronger profitability offset NXP's comparatively conservative financial policies. When compared with Marvell Technology Group Ltd. (BBB-/Stable), Broadcom's financial structure should remain less conservative despite more recent partially debt-funded acquisitions by Marvell.

Key Assumptions

--Organic revenue is up by mid-teens in fiscal 2022, driven by broad-based demand strength and constructive pricing environment, followed by a macro-economic fueled downturn in fiscal 2023;

--Broadcom closes the VMware acquisition at the end of fiscal 2023 and VMware adds mid-single digit revenue growth to Broadcom's low- to mid-single digit revenue growth through the remainder of the forecast period. This excludes meaningful contributions from revenue cross-selling actions;

--Broadcom's organic operating EBITDA margins remain in the low-60% in fiscal 2022 and decline slightly in fiscal 2023 as the result of lower revenue levels;

--The addition of VMware's mid-30% operating EBITDA margins are dilutive to Broadcom's organic operating EBITDA margins near-60% but cost actions drive profit margins expansion from the low- to mid-50% through the forecast period;

--Acquisition adds $32 billion of new debt in addition to VMware's $8 billion of pro forma existing net debt. Modest positive organic growth through the forecast period, given focus on legacy software assets and diversified semiconductor product offerings;

--Broadcom repurchases $5 billion of common shares over fiscal 2022 and 2023 before shifting to prioritize the same amount of debt reduction, while dividends continue to grow by 10% annually.

The ratings and outlook reflect Broadcom's meaningful revenue diversification with an increasing mix of recurring software revenue, strong positions in focus markets and semiconductor sector leading profit margins. Key Rating Drivers

Acquisitive Nature: Fitch expects Broadcom will remain acquisitive in the infrastructure software space, given still considerable industry fragmentation and lower political and regulatory risk compared with that of semiconductors. The company's track record suggests that mainly debt-financed mid- to large-scale acquisitions are more likely than organically funded tuck-in deals. In a leverage-neutral transaction, Broadcom will offer to exchange up to $2 billion of new notes due 2033 and up to $3 billion of notes due 2034 plus a cash payment for a first pool of existing senior notes with 2025 maturities and a second pool of existing notes with 2024, 2026 and 2027 maturities, respectively.

The rating and Outlook are constrained by Fitch's expectation that Broadcom will remain acquisitive in the software space, likely requiring at least partial debt funding and limiting meaningful credit metric strengthening.

The proposed exchange consists of Broadcom offering up to $2.0 billion of new notes due 2033 in exchange for, in order of priority, Broadcom Corp.'s $1.0 billion of 3.125% notes and the company's $2.25 billion of each of 4.7% and 3.15% notes. The exchange notes will be pari passu with Broadcom's existing senior unsecured obligations, including full and unconditional guarantees by Broadcom Technologies, Inc. and Broadcom Corp. on an unsecured and unsubordinated basis.

Key Rating Drivers

Acquisitive Nature: Fitch expects Broadcom will remain acquisitive in the infrastructure software space, given still considerable industry fragmentation and lower political and regulatory risk compared with that of semiconductors. The company's track record suggests that at least partially debt-financed mid- to large-scale acquisitions are more likely than organically funded tuck-in deals. Nonetheless, Fitch expects Broadcom to use post-deal FCF to return credit metrics to within long-term ranges consistent with investment grade ratings over the subsequent 12-24 months.

Growth Profile: After stronger than forecasted operating performance in fiscal 2020, Fitch expects another year of solid organic revenue growth for Broadcom driven by end markets recovering from the onset of the coronavirus pandemic and supply constraints that have lengthened lead times since last year. Double bookings remain a concern for the semiconductor sector, but Fitch believes broad-based recovery will augment secular semiconductor content growth, and Broadcom's all-you-can-eat software model over the near term.

Broadcom's software acquisitions also increased predictability of profiles, given considerable recurring maintenance and support revenue.

Broadcom's financial structure remains weak for the 'BBB-' rating on an operating EBITDA basis due to recent debt-financed acquisitions, but strong for the rating on a cash flow basis in part due to the company's low capital intensity. The ratings and Outlook reflect Broadcom's software assets, which now account for more than one-third of consolidated revenue and continue to add stability with recurring maintenance revenue at lower growth levels but higher profit margins. Demand trends for Broadcom's semiconductor franchises are mixed with 5G- and cloud-related businesses continuing to grow organically, but offset by those serving macro-economic sensitive markets such as satellite and set-top boxes.
